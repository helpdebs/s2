📍 Full transcript (Level 1):
“The thing is, we had a non-conformance in the heat exchanger after just 18 months. What happened was the client changed the operating temperature, but they didn’t inform us. At first, I thought it was pitting corrosion due to chlorides. It turned out that the real issue was caused by a combination of higher flow velocity and low pH. What made it difficult was the lack of historical data – I ended up re-running the whole root cause analysis. Long story short, the corrosion rate increased by 40%. I was supposed to present the findings last week, but I had to escalate the issue to the metallurgy team. They told me that the failure mechanism was stress corrosion cracking. Initially, I felt awkward because I had misunderstood the client’s specs. In the end, we realized that we need mitigation measures and better communication. I’m used to working under pressure, but this time I had a doubt about the sampling procedure. They don't always explain the context, so I have no way of knowing the full history. Anyway, everything worked out after we re-simulated the conditions.”
